I was raised in Auburn, Washington, and went to Auburn High. It was not until the last semester of my Senior year at the University of Puget Sound that I discovered pottery. I was drawn in by the wheel, and the tangible results that were available. Ken Stevens was my first instructor.

In 2007 I returned to ceramics, taking classes at Green River Community College. I took classes from Ed Brannon, John A. Taylor, and Paul Metivier. I also learned a great deal from the Instructional Tech, Vic Rivet. It was at Green River that I really started to get a feel for clay and how to fire a gas kiln to high temperatures.

In 2010 I left Green River and started working with Elinor Maroney at her studio in Covington, Washington. Elinor has been working in clay for more than 45 years and is a wonderful artist. I have learned immensely from Elinor, and in turn I assist with the heavy lifting. I constructed a soda kiln at the studio, so we could experiment with atmospheric glazing and learn the fine details of this challenging process.

I am now getting settled into my new studio in Tacoma, the Updraft Pottery Studio. While there are always improvements to be made to a studio, this one now has the right electrical to run kilns, wheels, and lights- at the same time. I am excited to put my new tools to the test, and continue the experiments!
