I was raised in Auburn, Washington, and went to Auburn High. It was not until the last semester of my Senior year at the University of Puget Sound that I tried pottery. I was drawn in by the wheel, and the tangible results that were available. Ken Stevens was my first professor. I came back the next summer to take another throwing class at UPS.

In 2007 I returned to ceramics, taking classes at Green River Community College. I took classes from Ed Brannon, John A. Taylor, and Paul Metivier. I also learned a great deal from the Instructional Tech, Vic Rivet. It was at Green River that I really started to get a feel for clay and how to fire a gas kiln to high temperatures.

In 2010 I left Green River and started working with Elinor Maroney at her Studio in Covington, Washington. Elinor has been working in clay for more than 45 years and is a wonderful artist. I have learned immensely from Elinor, and in turn I assist with the heavy lifting. I am refining the design of a new soda kiln at the Studio, so we can experiment with atmospheric glazing and learn the fine details of this challenging process.
